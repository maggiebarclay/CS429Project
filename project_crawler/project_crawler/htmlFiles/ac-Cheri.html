Cheri is a peppy bear cub villager in the Animal Crossing series. She first appeared in Animal Crossing and has appeared in all subsequent games except Animal Crossing: Wild World and Animal Crossing: City Folk. Cheri is one of two non-islander villagers who appear in Animal Crossing but not Doubutsu no Mori, the other being Punchy. Her name is derived from the French word chéri, meaning "dear" or "sweet," but may also come from cherry, referring to her fur color.

In New Horizons, Cheri has the fashion hobby and may be seen wearing a pink purse with a white flower on it, as well as a specific headwear or accessory item.

Characteristics[edit]

Artwork of Cheri from Animal Crossing.

Appearance[edit]

Cheri is a red bear cub with a white muzzle. She has a faint yellow coloring inside her ears. She has eyebrows that make her look like she is mad and has white tips on her paws. She also has darker red hair on her head, and her eyebrows are dark red as well.

Personality[edit]

Below is a brief description of the peppy personality. For more information, click here.

As a peppy villager, Cheri has the tendency to over-react in conversations about trivial subjects such as appearing in competitions and the chance of her losing. This sometimes triggers her to ask for a certain item of clothing or furniture which she needs to enter a competition. She, like other peppy villagers, is rarely discouraged from doing anything, including the usual hobbies. She also has a short attention span, which means she soon forgets arguments or tasks given to the player that are incomplete. She may ask the player, "Sooo, how am I dressed today?" The player is presented with a slide-bar indicating Kinda slobby... and Super formal! Moving the bar close to Super formal! will make her happy.

    Trivia[edit]

    • Cheri appears on the album covers of "I Love You" and "K.K. Faire."
      • The e-Reader card artwork of "K.K. Faire" originally displayed June, another bear cub villager, in place of Cheri.
    • In a character popularity poll held on the Japanese Animal Crossing website, Cheri ranked as the 30th most popular character at 609 votes, higher than Stella, but lower than Bunnie.
